Driver services

Emergency contacts

Designed to be readable quickly when something goes wrong on the road.

999

Emergency police, ambulance or fire. Use when there is immediate danger.

101

Non-emergency police reporting and advice.

111

NHS non-emergency medical advice.

National Highways

Motorway and major A-road information in England.

Accident checklist

  1. Stop safely and switch on hazard lights.
  2. Check for injuries and call 999 if needed.
  3. Exchange details with other drivers.
  4. Take photos of vehicles, road position and damage.
  5. Contact your insurer or breakdown provider.
Tip: Add your insurer, breakdown provider and emergency contact to your phone favourites.
