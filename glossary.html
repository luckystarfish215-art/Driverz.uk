Glossary

UK driving terms, explained simply

Short definitions for common fuel, EV and UK driving terms.

Fuel terms

E10Regular unleaded petrol containing up to 10% renewable ethanol.
E5Super unleaded petrol containing up to 5% ethanol. Often used by older or performance vehicles.
B7Standard diesel containing up to 7% biodiesel.
pplPence per litre, the normal way UK fuel prices are displayed.

EV terms

Type 2A common AC charging connector used by many UK and European EVs.
CCSA common rapid DC charging connector for many modern EVs.
CHAdeMOA rapid DC connector used by some older EVs.
kWhKilowatt-hour, the unit used for battery capacity and EV charging energy.
p/kWhPence per kilowatt-hour, a common EV charging price format.

Road and service terms

MOTThe annual roadworthiness test required for most vehicles over three years old.
VEDVehicle Excise Duty, commonly called road tax or vehicle tax.
CAZClean Air Zone. Some vehicles may need to pay to enter certain zones.
